Coffee Roasting Notes

The following roasts were all completed in a cheap popcorn roaster purchased from target on special for AU$4.
Essentially this is a heating element and a fan, with the inlets directed around the roasting chamber creating a hot vortex, that heats and stirs the beans.
Volumes need to be kept relatively small to prevent clogging up and preventing the beans from circulating freely.

09/04/15

75g No timer No Stirring
Excessive chaff - roast stopped part way. Future roasts to be done outside!!

10/04/15

First batch

75g 2:45 No Stirring
Slightly darker than first, still a little underdone. Consistency of roast not fantastic.

Second batch

75g 3:30 Stirred
Beans agitated with wooden spoon to try to give better roast consistency.
First crack took mch longer than the first due to the agitation.
Slight improvement in consistency.
Still tasted green after sitting over a week.

10/05/15

First Batch

75g 3:30 No Stirring
Crack very widely spaced out. Very inconsistent roast.

Second Batch

40g 3:30 No Stirring
Big improvement in mixing. Much more consistent roast. Very Dark.
Palatable, sweet aroma. Some oils lost from the bean, transferred onto paper used as label, suggesting slightly overdone.

Third Batch

40g 3:00 No Stirring
Similar consistency to Second batch. Roast depth midway between First and Second batches.
Strong charred flavour. Perhaps from buildup of oil from previous roasts?

Fourth Batch

26g 2:45 No Stirring
Mix up with scales. Wrong weight measured out. Very similar to third batch.
